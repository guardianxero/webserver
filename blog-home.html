Facebook NorCal Hackathon

by Stephan Liu

Posted on October 14, 2013 at 10:45 PM

It's been a month since I started as an EECS major at Berekeley, and I had always been hearing about these fabled hackathons, where one goes to program day and night and end up with some magical program. Well, Facebook hosted one last weekend at their Menlo Park Headquarters, and sure enough, I jumped on this opportunity with a few of my friends and attended this gathering of nerds (yes, I'm and nerd and I'm PROUD OF IT). As this was my first hackathon, I wasn't quite sure what was going on, but my friends and I decided on writing a Google Chrome extension and disregarded the fact that none of use had experience with Javascript. Starting from nothing, we treaded through Javascript documentation, tested various Chrome API implementations, and spent hours debugging code. But we indeed made it to the end, and we had a prototype of an extension that would autosort open tabs while giving you notifications of updates on those open tabs. While it's not perfect, as many would say, "Something is always better than nothing!"
